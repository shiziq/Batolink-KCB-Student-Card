Section 2 — The Problem

Challenges Facing
Kenya's Youth Today

Three distinct segments. Very different lives. One common thread: the financial system is failing them.

🏫
Age 16 – 18
High School Students
  • Carry cash to school daily — constant safety risk and theft target
  • No financial literacy or savings habits formed at this critical age
  • Parents have zero visibility on how money is actually spent
  • Schools cannot accurately track or manage canteen revenue
  • No digital payment infrastructure exists on any secondary school campus
🎓
Age 18 – 24
University Students
  • Frequently run out of money mid-semester with no safety net
  • Poor budgeting leads to hunger, missed meals and exam stress
  • Parents send money via M-Pesa with zero spending visibility
  • Emergency expenses go unpaid — medical bills, registration fees
  • No access to credit, micro-lending, or any financial products
💼
Age 22 – 30
Entry-Level Professionals
  • No credit history prevents access to formal KCB lending products
  • Side hustle income is informal and completely untracked by any bank
  • Banks don't engage them early enough to build trust or loyalty
  • Largely excluded from mainstream KCB products and services
  • Massive untapped potential for deposit mobilization at KCB
